## Author’s Guidelines: 

We invite any members of the research community to have their Jupyter notebooks published on the Impresso Datalab website. Notebooks should engage with at least one of the following: 
- Impresso corpus;
- Impresso models;
- Impresso API; or
- Impresso Python library. 

Impresso Datalab notebooks can be prepared as:
- Tutorials (or learning material) engaging with Impresso project outputs;
- Demos of a tool (or method) to explore the Impresso corpus, visualise historical newspapers’ data or enrich external data using Impresso models; 
- Research-based Jupyter notebook, structured around a historical research question. These notebooks aim to inspire researchers to use Impresso materials in a creative manner. 

Ideas for new notebooks should be sent via email to info@impresso-project.ch. The editors of the Datalab editorial board will provide you with feedback and guidance on how to get your draft prepared and submitted for peer-review. 

### Important files:
- [Datalab editorial pipeline](https://github.com/impresso/impresso-datalab-notebooks/blob/main/documentation/editorial-pipeline-overview.md)
Impresso Datalab notebooks go through peer-review before they are made publicly available on GitHub and on the Datalab website. We strive to produce materials that are intelligible; accessible to a wide audience with diverse coding skills levels; sustainable and informative. The peer-review process, therefore, assists with achieving these goals. Datalab peer-review is open and conducted via GitHub issues. This helps us ensure transparency of the reviewing process as well as facilitate documentation of feedback and changes to the document (version control).
